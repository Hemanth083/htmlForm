JOB DESCRIPTION

TITLE OF POSITION: DIRECT CARE WORKER / ATTENDANT CARE
TITLE OF IMMEDIATE SUPERVISOR: ATC/DCW SUPERVISOR

DUTIES OF POSITION

1. Provides personal care and related services in the home, under the direction, instruction and supervision of the ATC/DCW Supervisor.

2. Tasks to be performed by an ATC/DCW must be assigned by and performed as per the POC/ISP/ISP.

3. Under no circumstances may an ATC/DCW be assigned to receive or reduce any intravenous procedures, procedures involving the use of Levin's tubes or Foley catheters, or any other sterile or invasive procedures, other than rectal temperatures or enemas.

POSITION RESPONSIBILITIES

1. Follows the POC/ISP to provide, safe, competent care to the client.

2. Helps the client to maintain good personal hygiene and assists in maintaining a healthful, safe environment.

3. Plans and prepares nutritious meals, markets as per POC/ISP.

4. Assists the patient with ambulation as per POC/ISP.

5. Assists the therapy personnel as needed with rehabilitative processes.

6. Encourages the client to become as independent as possible according to the POC/ISP.

7. Attempts to promote client’s mental alertness through involvement in activities of interest.

8. Gives simple emotional and psychological support to the patient and other members of the household and relationship with client and family which transmit trust and establishes a confidentiality.

9. Reports any change in the client's mental or physical condition or in the home situation to the ATC/DCW Supervisor.

10. Performs routine housekeeping tasks as related to a safe and comfortable environment for the client.

11. Prepares a visit report promptly and incorporates same in the clinical record weekly.

12. Confirms on a weekly basis, the scheduling of visits so that other necessary visits by staff members can be coordinated.

13. Works with personnel of other community agencies involved in the patient's care as directed by the ATC/DCW Supervisor.

14. Attends in-service as required by regulation.

JOB CONDITIONS

1. The ability to drive and the ability to access clients’ homes which may not be routinely wheelchair accessible are required.

2. Hearing, eyesight and physical dexterity must be sufficient to perform a physical assessment of the client's condition and to perform client care.

3. On occasion, may be required to bend, stoop, reach and move client weight up to 250 pounds; lift and/or carry up to 30 pounds.

4. Must be able to communicate clearly, both verbally and in writing.

5. Must be able to administer the medications listed on the POC/ISP to the client, report any new or changed medications to the supervisor.

COMPANY INFORMATION

Has access to all client specific task list and EVV which may be discussed with the ATC/DCW Supervisor and the Director of Nursing

QUALIFICATIONS

1. Preferably a high school diploma or equivalent ain.

2. Must have completed Article 9 & DCW Training requirements within 90 days of hire if no proof of previous trainings provided.

3. Must be free from health problems that magebe injurious to patient, self and co-workers and must present appropriate evidence ta,substantiate this

4. Must comprehends the basics of personal care, housekeeping and meal preparation

5. Must understand and respect client's including ethics and confidentiality of care

6. CNA, RN, LPN are exempt from DCW Training and Testing.
